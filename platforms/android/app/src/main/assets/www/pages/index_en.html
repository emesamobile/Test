La cata del Barrio La Estacion

The Railway Station District in Haro (La Rioja, Spain) is a unique place with over 150 years’ history. It is a district where great wines have been made from the middle of the 19th century to the 21st century, preserving their essence, keeping their magic.

Something very special is going to happen in the Station District and we would like you to join us… Let’s synchronize our watches!

Next stop: Haro Station District.

El Barrio de la Estación

El Barrio de la Estación is an emblematic place in the city of Haro, an enclave that presents the highest concentration of centuries-old wineries in the world and whose construction is a central and common axis: the railway station. Hence it is known as the golden mile of Rioja wine.

But that the neighbors of the neighborhood are centuries-old wineries does not mean that they are old, because the wineries are constructions that are never finished, generation after generation evolve and manage to mix their most classic part, the oldest, with the most modern architecture.

In this way, a visit to the Haro Station Neighborhood becomes a journey through time, where you can follow the evolution of wine and architecture of the last century.
